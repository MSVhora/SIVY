The internet has opened up a whole new world of marketing opportunities for hotels and businesses in the travel and tourism industry. Traditional ways of marketing hotels are no more effective when your aim is to attract visitors from across the world.

Smart hotel marketers have already chosen internet as their preferred marketing medium. The scalability, cost-effectiveness and faster execution of ideas make the internet just perfect for marketing hotels.

If your hotel business hasn’t taken the online leap yet, this is the time to do so. Let us explain how you can leverage the internet medium in the best possible manner:

Improved website design:

Your hotel’s website often creates the first impression of your property to a potential visitor. So it is important that you pull out all stops to ensure that the visitor develops a favorable attitude towards your hotel. This means that your website must have meaningful content, should load fast, have excellent navigation, updated video and photo galleries and even interactive features like virtual tour and location map. Make sure your website layout is uncluttered, elegant and content rich.

Go mobile:

Mobile device adoption is clearly on the rise and it is vital that your website is also responsive to mobile and tablet devices. Your mobile website needs to be straightforward, so that the most important information is displayed right at the top and unnecessary clutter is avoided. Responsive Web Design technology may be used to ensure that an optimal viewing experience across a wide range of devices like desktops, smartphones and tablets. What’s more, even Google recommends responsive websites.

Ride the social media wave:

The social media movement is here to stay and your hotel can reap all its benefit by establishing a powerful presence on social media channels like Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest, Google + and YouTube. For example, you can share pictures of your hotel on your hotel’s Pinterest channel which will get repinned by your followers and thus introduce your hotel to more potential visitors. Reputation management is an important factor to be kept in mind while engaging via social media channels.  By creating an active social media campaign on Facebook and Twitter, you can keep people aware of happenings in your hotel and in your region.

Adopt paid advertising:

If you have the marketing budget or need quick results, don’t forget to include well conceived and positioned paid ads in your online media campaign. Digital advertising services like Google Ads and Facebook Ads offer a variety of advantages like precise targeting and estimation capabilities that traditional ads do not offer. For example, with Google Ads, you can ensure that your ads are displayed at those places where they are likely to be seen by the very people they are targeted at. For example, if your hotel has a golf course, Google can display your ads in websites, blogs and forums that discuss golf, thereby bringing your hotel in front of a targeted crowd.

Digital advertising also offers the facility of re-targeting, i.e., advertising to a consumer who has shown some sort of engagement with the brand, especially when the previous case did not result in a conversion. This way, it becomes possible to leverage on the earlier marketing that took the consumer to the previous brand’s site in the first place and thus, increase chances of a future conversion taking place.

Present yourself on popular travel portals:

Make sure that your hotel is listed on popular travel portals like TripAdvisor, Expedia, Booking.com, etc. Not only does this help improve your visibility to visitors from across the world, it also helps you tap into the traffic generated by these portals, especially if your hotel is one of the major accommodation facilities in your area.

Make sure your hotel is also listed in Google Hotels, which is currently being majorly promoted by Google.

If your hotel is known for its restaurants, make sure they are listed on leading restaurant review sites like Yelp.

Reviews:

For every hotel, getting positive reviews on travel portals and social channels is extremely important. Make sure you take every effort in getting positive, descriptive feedback from your customers. You can vastly improve your review count with simple steps like sending a thank you note to the customer with direct links to the review pages and specifically requesting them to post their experience.

Make sure to check back regularly on your review pages and respond to any negative comments quickly and compassionately.

Local optimization:

When Google detects that your search query carries local intent, it provides a list of business listings relating to your query that are located near you. For example, if you search for “hotels in New York”, Google will give you business listings of popular hotels in New York along with their NPA (name, phone number and address).  It is important that you optimize local search to the fullest extent as it gives you a significant advantage over other bigger hotels. Let’s put it like this- the bigger hotels may have a bigger website and the best SEO tactics in place, but your location will give you the edge that you need to make sure that the user zeroes in on your hotel.

Video optimization:

In order to improve your site’s visibility online, you can upload videos of your hotel and its offerings to your site which not only give your potential visitors a better idea of the overall décor of your hotel and the services offered, but also help bring more visitors to your site by the use of appropriate SEO tactics. For example, you can upload a few videos to YouTube and attach a few links to your hotel’s website. This way, if the user likes what he sees in the video, he can conveniently click on the link and be taken to your website.

In addition, if your hotel is based in a city that is a popular tourist spot, you can take videos of the tourist attractions in your city and upload them to your hotel website, thus promoting your hotel indirectly.

If your website includes streaming videos, don’t forget to employ effective video optimization tactics, including having a video sitemap.

E-commerce integration:

Make sure to make the online booking facility in your hotel website simple, seamless and secure. Online booking is a very convenient way for visitors to book their stay with minimum effort on their part. A large chunk of hotel bookings nowadays is made online and you need to ensure that you don’t lose out on this promising opportunity to increase hotel occupancy.

Marketing through external blogs:

External blog marketing can be of many types: either you place ads on blogs relevant to your subject, or you could write guest posts on other influential travel blogs. From a hotel’s point of view, it makes sense to place ads for your hotel on popular hotel blogs. As a respected hotelier, you can even offer to write a guest post on a popular hotel blog in which you may give your views about the hospitality industry. While writing your guest post, don’t forget to include a link to your hotel website! You can also reach out to popular bloggers to mention or review your hotel on their blogs. An approval by a respected and popular blogger can translate into a significantly higher number of visitors.

Email marketing:

Creating a robust email database of your customers is an excellent way to stay in touch with them and let them know about new offers at your hotel. Another excellent tactic would be to send them creatively designed e-cards on special occasions like birthday, anniversary or during religious festivals.

Conclusion:

The above are some of the tactics that will go a great way in enhancing your hotel’s online presence. What is important is to use the internet medium effectively by keeping your audience consistently engaged in a creative, honest way. This will not just improve your hotel’s reputation online, but encourage visitors to recommend your hotel to their peers.

The whole point of having a website is to make it visible across the web and appeal to target visitors. If your website is not indexed, it won’t appear in the search results and this defeats the purpose for which it was created in the first place. There might be a lot of reasons behind search engines not indexing your website. Here is the checklist of common problems related to site indexing and the solutions to fix them.
 

Robots.txt

Robots.txt, if present, is the first thing that a search engine bot looks up when visiting your website. It is used by webmasters to provide instructions to the search engine bots visiting a site. If your robots.txt file carried instructions to discourage search engine crawling, your site may not get indexed. You can check your site’s robots file by adding /robots.txt at the end of your domain name.

If you see the above instruction in your robots.txt file, it means that the search engine bots are discouraged from crawling your site’s content. You can fix this issue easily by removing the ‘/’ from this command. You can use the robots.txt file effectively to de-index specific folders in your site. Google’s very own robots file has covered an extensive list of commands. Check it out for your reference.

Meta Tags

It is a common practice by webmasters to use meta ‘robots’ tag to prevent search engine bots from crawling a particular web page. If a particular page in your website doesn’t get indexed, check its source code for this meta tag:

This tag will prevent the web page from being crawled by the search engine bots. Some WordPress themes incorporate this meta tag by default. If your WP site has indexing problems, it is worth checking the theme header for this tag. Once this tag is removed from the source code, search engine bots can crawl the particular web page.

Sitemap Errors

A sitemap (XML or HTML) provides a search engine robot with the list of URLs to be crawled in your website. If for some reasons the sitemap in your website is not automatically updated, search engine bots may some time to discover those webpages and this would cause delay in indexing.

You need to check out the Webmaster tool (set up a Google Webmaster account for your site if you don’t already have it) for issues related to the sitemap. The Sitemap Details page will inform you about the errors in it. Once the errors are fixed, you can generate a new xml sitemap for your site and upload it in the root for your website.

Other Crawling Errors

Once you’ve ruled out the above mentioned problems, head to your Google Webmaster Tools dashboard and check for crawling errors. The crawling errors need to be fixed as and when you see them. Some of the page crawling errors might be related URL parameter issues, which mostly happen in the case of dynamic links. You can fix errors like this by using a simple 301 redirect.

Getting Indexed Under a Different Domain Name

Before coming to a conclusion that your website is not indexed, check out for other variations in your domain name that might have been indexed by search engines. For instance, http://www.yoursite.com and http://yoursite.com might look similar to you. However, they are two different URLs according to the search engines.

If you website is indexed in a different name than the one you preferred, you need to specify your preferred domain name in the Webmaster tools and use a 301 redirect. You can also use canonical link element for setting the most preferred URL version but a 301 redirect is more effective.

No Incoming Links

This issue is common in websites that are new to the web. If your website doesn’t have any incoming links from other websites, it might take a while for Google to find your site. If you wish to get your site indexed soon, you may have to build some backlinks manually. Backlinks from high PR websites will provide more link weightage to your website thereby helping it get indexed soon.

Submitting your site to Google Plus will earn you an incoming link and help your site get discovered by the search engine bots. You can also submit your site URL to Google through your Webmasters tool. These methods will significantly improve the chances of getting indexed by Google.

AJAX Applications

If your website runs AJAX application, it would contain AJAX URLs (a URL with # fragment) and the content of these URLs can’t be crawled by search engine bots. This, in turn, would prevent the search engines from indexing your website. There is a separate process to help search engine bots crawl AJAX content. The solution provided by Google Developers will help you fix this issue.

Bad Links & Google Reconsideration Request

It is important for a site owner to constantly assess the quality of incoming links. The inbound links to your website should not violate Google’s guidelines on link quality. You can detect these bad links through your Google Webmaster Tools. If your site has too many bad links, it will be de-indexed by Google and other search engines.

If by any chance your website has been de-indexed due to this issue, you need to disavow these bad links completely and make sure that your site no longer violates the guidelines. Once this is fixed, you need to submit a reconsideration request to Google through your Webmaster tools.

SEO Spamming

There are many black hat SEO techniques practiced by spammers to get higher rankings instantly. Google constantly keeps tab of its search results quality and takes manual action on sites that employ spamming techniques. Shortcut methods like cloaking, keyword stuffing, content scraping and spam links etc. will get your site permanently de-indexed.

The simple solution here is to clean up your site of all the spamming techniques and submit a reconsideration request through your Webmaster tools.

Malware Ridden Websites

Websites infected with malwares may get disappeared from search results very soon. The silver lining here is that you can find the issue before your site gets de-indexed. Google Webmaster Tools will issue a warning that your site has been compromised. Once you’ve discovered the issue, you can remove the malware and enhance your site’s security. You can also submit a reconsideration request if your site has already been de-indexed.

Website Downtime

If your website is continuously down for a significant period (say a few weeks) of time, it might get de-indexed from the search engines. The only solution here is to fix the issue causing downtime and get your site back online. Once your site is back online, it may take a while to get your traffic back to its normal status. If your site isn’t indexed even after fixing the issue, you may have to submit the reconsideration request in the Webmaster Tools.

In addition to the ones listed here, Google has also mentioned a few more cases in which your site may not get indexed.

Fetch as Google

If you believe your website doesn’t have any of the above issues, here’s a simple way to get your web page indexed: Log on to your Google Webmaster Tools account and click on ‘Fetch as Google’ link under ‘Crawl’. Enter your URL and submit to index. You may see the page indexed on Google within seconds. I do this every time I publish a new blog post and it works like a charm.  

Conclusion:

These are some of the common issues you need to check if your site hasn’t got indexed.  If you have experienced website indexing issues other than the ones listed here,  feel free to mention it in the comments below.

If you are a WordPress user, you might have come across a range of plugins that could enhance your website with new features. Plugins allow you to choose only the features you need most, rather than taxing your site with unwanted features. With the right combination of plugins, you can do almost anything in a WordPress website. Here is our pick of the top WordPress plugins that will make your WordPress website feature rich and amazingly functional. 

WordPress SEO Plugin by Yoast

The WordPress SEO plugin by Yoast is among the most widely used SEO plugin in WordPress. This plugin helps you keep your website optimized and it comes with many innovative features. You can optimize page titles, meta description, canonical URLs, breadcrumbs and XML sitemaps etc. using the Yoast SEO plugin. Furthermore, this plugin could also edit your robots.txt file and enhance your RSS feeds.

All in One SEO Pack

This is an alternative to the Yoast SEO plugin and it mostly performs similar functions. Some of the crucial on-page optimization factors like page title, meta description and meta keywords etc. can be optimized with the help of this plugin. This plugin can also help you avoid duplicate content on your WordPress blog.

WP Open Graph

This plugin lets you add Facebook meta data for any of your posts. While the plugin automatically extracts the meta data from the SEO plugins you’ve installed, you also have the option of editing this data and customizing them specific to each posts.

Disqus Comment System

Comments are essential for getting feedback from visitors and improving interactions on your blog. As a site owner, it is your duty to provide a solid platform for your visitors to participate in discussions and knowledge sharing. Disqus is a commenting tool that makes it easier for visitors to interact. By default, Disqus makes all external links in the comments ‘nofollow’ thereby dissuading spammers from posting their links. You also have the power to approve, reject or block comments.

Hello Bar Plugin

Hello Bar plugin resides on the extreme top of your blog page and is another innovative tool that allows you to direct your traffic to important pages of your website. When the target visitors get driven to that particular page, it could result in more conversions. Hello Bar also provides you nifty analytics on the number of clicks. Though not a completely free tool, the basic free version should be good to start with.

WP Related Posts Plugin

This WordPress plugin drives user engagement by displaying a string of related posts. The related posts are grouped together based on their matching topics and they get displayed at the bottom of a post. This is a critical factor in making users stick to your blog and browse multiple pages upon every visit.

WordPress Backup to Dropbox

Worried about losing your website content? WordPress Backup to Dropbox is there to help you. This is an effective plugin to create an automatic backup copy of your entire website. The content you regularly add to your website gets automatically saved in your Dropbox account. While large business websites need premium tools like BackupBuddy, the Dropbox plugin is ideal for small websites.

W3 Total Cache

This is a performance optimization plugin that caches various aspects of your website. The plugin promises up to 10 times improved site performance when it is fully configured. This plugin is also instrumental in reducing page load time despite sustaining high traffic periods.

WP Content Copy Protection

If you are worried about someone copying your website content, you can use the content copy protection plugin. This one discourages content theft by disabling some of the commonly used copying methods. Features like right click, drag and drop, hold to copy and keyboard shortcuts etc. can be disabled with the help of this plugin.

SEO Crawlytics

SEO Crawlytics isn’t just another SEO plugin; rather it is a tool that helps site owners understand the search engine bots visiting their site. Crucial information like most crawled pages, robot visit details, crawl history and peak crawl times etc. can be obtained through this plugin. You can use this information to optimize your site’s performance.

NextGEN Gallery

This is an extremely handy plugin, especially when your site has lots of images. The NextGEN Gallery plugin helps in managing images on your website. Features like batch upload, resizing and sorting etc. are possible with the help of this plugin. 

 It is worth noting that WordPress has thousands of plugins and the ones listed here are handpicked to be the most useful ones for almost any website. Depending upon your specific needs, you may also require a few other plugins in addition to the ones given above. Google launched the Chromecast, which is expected to revolutionize the world of media streaming. The Google Chromecast is a simple adapter that can be plugged into the HDMI port of a television.
